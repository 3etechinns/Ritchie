Legal

Ritchie is published under the Lesser Gnu Public License (LGPL), colloquially known as a "copyleft."

In layman's terms:

  • You may trade and make a profit on the Ritchie Stock Exchange, using the Ritchie API and adapter, without publishing your algorithms. In other words, you are not required to make public your algos, quantitative methods, strategies, or any other information of this nature.
  • There is a clear separation of concerns: your proprietary stuff will run on equipment separate from Ritchie (either on a shared server or colocated equipment) and be physically separate from the Ritchie exchange.
  • On the other hand, you must make public work that you do on the Ritchie API, server, or adapter.

Examples:

  1. You write a profitable algorithm to analyze price movement based on an advanced probabilistic model. This algo runs on your colocated equipment, and interfaces with Ritchie through the API. You are not required to make this code public.
  2. You check out the Ritchie API from Github, and write a method called straddle(), which enables users to create a financial option straddle order (e.g., two legs) with one function call. You are required to make this public.
